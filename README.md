## Hi, I'm Julian

I build machine learning systems that turn complex biomedical data into actionable insight, creating “move 37” moments: AI-enabled, creatively disruptive solutions to hard problems.

**What I work on:** My work combines applied ML, deep learning engineering, and healthcare AI. During my MSc at UBC, I designed and ran end-to-end ML pipelines in Python for multimodal 3D brain imaging and structured clinical data from thousands of participants in one of the world's largest developmental clinical datasets (the ABCD Study). I implemented and fine-tuned Vision Transformer architectures for 3D MRI classification in a high-risk population (including distributed GPU training on HPC infrastructure), applied semi-supervised clustering to discover data-driven subtypes, and built baseline predictive models on curated neuroimaging features.

**Some of my work:** My flagship portfolio project, brain-risk-hybrid-ML-engine, translates this research into a production-style open-source repository: modular Python packaging (src layout, pyproject.toml, CLI entrypoints), config-driven ML workflows, synthetic-data demos for reproducibility, end-to-end preprocessing pipelines, clustering and deep learning modules, testing, CI/CD via GitHub Actions, Docker, and HPC/SLURM/DDP documentation.

**Currently building:** A GenAI and LLM system: a healthcare-focused RAG service and evaluation suite (with LLMOps, retrieval evaluation, and observability).

**Expertise:** Strongest in medical AI, clinical AI, digital health, pharma, and biotech. Excited by opportunities and collaborations in high-impact domains where complex data meets high-stakes decisions, and where rigorous modeling and production-grade systems matter.
